The rules

The basic rule is identical to the other popular falling-piece type puzzle games, which means that pieces are disappeared when they form a group of more than 4 pieces and if the group consists entirely of the same piece type.
And your goal is to earn as much points as possible, it's also common to other puzzle games.
In addition to that, in Yurupoyo it applies an original rule called "coupling" or maybe "pairing"(although either way is possible I prefer to coupling).
Coupling happens when two groups of 3 diffrent pieces become neighbors and even though coupling earns you less points than the normal four-pieces pairs, it'll show you some eye-candies if you are familliar with "Yuruyuri".

The more words a sentence has, the more complicated it becomes, I guess, right? So I'll just show you a few pictures and they'll surely explain what coupling is.
Then look at Fig.1, and guess where a coupling happens.

Himawari(the blue pieces at middle-center) and Sakurako(the yellow pieces below them) form groups of three and are next to each other, they are in coupling.
What about Fig.2?

In Fig.2 Yui(the black pieces at middle-upper) and Akari(the red pieces in lower-right corner) make groups of three pieces but they are NOT next to each other and therefore aren't in coupling.
And then let's stick to Fig.3!

This time around Himawari, Sakurako and Chitose(the gray pieces in upper-right corner) are adjacent to each other but any of the two groups don't have both 3 members(one has 3 and the others 2) and again they are not in coupling.
